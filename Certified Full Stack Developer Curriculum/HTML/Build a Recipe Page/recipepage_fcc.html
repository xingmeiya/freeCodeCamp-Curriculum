Air Fryer Miso Salmon w/Veggies and Rice

This recipe is inspired by Tiffy Cooks's recipe for Air Fryer Miso Glazed Salmon.

Ingredients

  • 4 salmon fillets (deboned and descaled)
  • 3 tbsp. white miso paste
  • 3 tbsp. Kewpie Mayo/regular mayo
  • 1 tbsp. mirin (honey or sugar can be used as a substitute)
  • 2 tbsp. soy sauce (Kikkoman brand or other Japanese soy sauce brand preferred)
  • 1 tbsp. Hon Dashi powder
  • Black pepper to taste
  • OPTIONAL: Huy Fong chili paste/your favorite chili paste or hot sauce to taste
  • 1 baby bok choy
  • 1 large carrot
  • 1 large handful of fresh/frozen broccoli
  • 2 cups of uncooked white rice (short grain or medium grain)

Appliances & Other Tools Needed

  • Air fryer
  • Rice cooker/pot (optional)
  • Stove (optional)
  • Small mixing bowl
  • Measuring cups
  • Measuring spoons
  • Aluminum foil
  • Bowl/plate for serving
  • Kitchen knife
  • Utensils of choice (chopsticks, fork, spoon, etc.)

Instructions

  1. Descale salmon fillets with a kitchen knife or a fish scaler.
  2. In a mixing bowl, mix the white miso paste, soy sauce, mayo, mirin, dashi powder, and black pepper until well combined.
  3. Place the salmon fillets in the mixing bowl and make sure each fillet is covered and coated evenly with the marinade.
  4. Place the bowl with the marinated salmon fillets in the fridge and wait for 25 minutes.
  5. While the salmon is marinating, wash all of the vegetables.
  6. Remove each leaf from the baby bok choy and cut the core in half when it has reached a size that is too small to use individually.
  7. Cut the ends of the carrot and dispose of them and cut the carrot into half-inch slices.
  8. If using fresh broccoli, cut the florets from the stem.
